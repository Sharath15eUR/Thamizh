The different standards of IEEE 802.11 PHY layer and their characteristics are,

1.802.11
	-freq : 2.4Ghz
	-max data rate : 2 Mbps
	-modulation : FHSS,DSSS,infrared
	-it was the first standard and now its not used

2. 802.11a
	-freq:5Ghz
	-max data rate : 54Mbps
	-Modulation : OFDM
	-Channel Width : 20 MHz
	-High speed and less interference due to higher frequency

3. 802.11b
	-freq: 2.4 Ghz
	-max data rate : 11 Mbps
	-modulation : DSSS
	-channel width : 22 MHz
	-Better range than 802.11a but slower and susceptible to interference due to low freq.

4. 802.11g
	-freq: 2.4Ghz
	-max data rate: 54 Mbps
	-modulation:OFDM(also supports DSSS)
	-channel width:20 MHz
	-also compatible with 802.11b and high throughput

5. 802.11n  - wifi 4
	-freq: 2.4 & 5Ghz (dual band)
	-max data rate:600Mbps
	-modulation:OFDM
	-Channel width: 20/40 MHz
	-Supports MIMO

6. 802.11ac  - wifi 5
	-freq: 5Ghz only
	-max data rate: 6.9Gbps
	-modulation:OFDM (256 QAM)
	-channel width: 20/40/80/160 Mhz
	- supports MIMO in downlink
7. 802.11ax - wifi 6/6E
	-freq: 2.4Ghz,5Ghz, 6Ghz
	-max data rate: 9.6Gbps
	-modulation : OFDM (1024 QAM)
	-channel width : upto 160 Mhz
	-Supports MU-MIMO(both uplink and downlink)
